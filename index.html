Web Development Immersive Teaching Assistant

General Assemblty

Upon finishing the course, I was offered the job to be a teaching assistant for the upcoming course. It was at this time when the curriculum would shift into focusing more on JavaScript, with an emphasis on Node, Express, ES6 and Angular. I was responsible for reviewing all the students work that they had to complete out of hours and providing constructive feedback on how they could improve. This would have to be documented and brought forward in weekly meetings to discuss the progression of students with the instructors, producers and outcomes managers.

I also was in charge of all morning homework reviews which would consist of students coming to the front of the class and presenting their homework from the night before. During this exercise, I would help with bug fixing or be coaching them on problems they had during the task, whilst asking questions on their approach. I also took charge in introducing and running the independent exercises that were given to students during the day, ensuring I was always there to help out or assist. Which followed with a review once the exercise had been completed.

Possibly the most challenging aspect of the role was the project time where students had a week to build a project for their portfolios. These were far more complex and it was important that I kept track of every student's progress and was on hand to help whenever needed. This was done by talking through the problem and discussing ways they could approach it to overcome the error or bug. Occasionally pair programming with students if needed.

After the course had finished, I was approached to continue in this role as they were impressed with how I had performed. It was in this course where I expressed interest in taking a couple of lessons during the week and increasing the amount of responsibility that I had, which I enjoyed.

Education

Web Development Immersive Student

General Assemblty

The Web Development Immersive full-time course at General Assembly was my very first introduction to coding, development and problem-solving. It really tested me both mentally and physically but I knew from the first couple of weeks that this was what I wanted to do as I enjoyed constant process never feeling like there wasn’t something new to learn or get better at.

The course consisted of 4 modules, the first, covering very basic Ruby logic and syntax along with HTML, CSS and Javascript. The second, focusing on backend frameworks Sinatra and Ruby on Rails. In the third module, we covered consuming 3rd party API’s using AJAX followed by the final module where we looked at a front-end framework called Backbone.js. Along the way, we also covered user authentication, image upload and testing.

During this time, I created three projects, two individual projects and one in a small team of three, each project was a week long and allowed us to consolidate and practice the content we had covered during the module. These projects were my most enjoyable part of the course as it allowed me to test myself to ensure I understood every topic that was covered and in some cases go beyond and implement things completely on my own. Overall, it was a great exercise to get an understanding of how I would be working in the industry day to day.
